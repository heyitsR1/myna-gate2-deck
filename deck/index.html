Yodha Labs (IT company) as a supply-side reference point, not a target user
  • Who was targeted: solo or near-solo Nepali SMB owners who personally handle all customer communications across Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Viber and TikTok — the owner IS the entire customer-service team.
  • Split by design: two interviews (skin clinic, dental clinic) stress-test the reply-accuracy/trust pillar; one (OglePeek) stress-tests the channel-volume/omnichannel pillar; the fifth reinforces that no automated assistant is in use anywhere in the sample.
03 / 19
User Analysis

Findings

Cross-interview patterns from the five-interview sample.

  • Zero delegation: all owners personally handle communications — no system stands between them and the customer.
  • Universal after-hours pressure, coped with differently: one grinds through backlog at 10 PM (dental), one draws a boundary but still caves (skin clinic), one built an internal tool (Yodha — not the target segment).
  • Two consistent adoption blockers: trust/accuracy (fear of wrong medical or pricing info) and cost (a NPR 80,000 custom chatbot quote was rejected as too expensive).
  • Channel volume alone is already draining: OglePeek's Instagram DM volume overwhelms one co-founder with no after-hours element required.
  • Estimated cost of the gap: the skin clinic owner estimates 4–5 lost bookings per week from unanswered after-hours messages.

"Someone will message at 7 or 8 PM... by morning they've already called another clinic."

— Skin & Aesthetics Clinic Owner, Lazimpat

The Solution

One inbox. Every channel. Answered in your voice.

Myna unifies Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, TikTok and Viber into a single threaded inbox, and drafts — or auto-sends — replies in the owner's own tone, escalating anything uncertain, high-value, or urgent straight to them.

  • One-time setup teaches Maya the owner's tone, FAQs, and business info.
  • Every incoming message, on any channel, lands in one place.
  • Low-risk FAQ replies can auto-send; anything risky is flagged for the owner.

Core Features · Must Have 1 of 4

Unified inbox view across channels

A single scrollable list of every conversation across WhatsApp, Instagram and Facebook (Viber and TikTok shown as "coming soon"), with a channel badge and read/unread state per row — so the owner checks one place instead of five apps.

Core Features · Must Have 2 of 4

Maya-drafted replies in conversation view

Inside each thread, Maya's drafted messages are visually distinct from owner-sent ones, with an inline "Approve & send" or "Edit" action — the owner stays in control while Maya does the drafting.

Core Features · Must Have 3 of 4

Owner tone/FAQ setup

A one-time setup screen — business info, a tone selector (friendly / formal / concise), and an editable FAQ list — that trains Maya to reply the way the owner actually would.

Core Features · Must Have 4 of 4

Channel connect flow

Fake "Connect" buttons for WhatsApp, Instagram and Facebook with a success state; Viber and TikTok appear as "Coming soon" pills, reflecting their evidence status honestly rather than overclaiming scope.

Meet The Mascot

Maya, the myna

A myna bird that literally learns the voices around it and speaks them back — the single most demo-able metaphor for something that replies in your voice. Restyled toward the merged brand's navy-and-brass system: deep navy/teal plumage, a brass-gold eye-ring signature, and a small clay/marigold chest-flash — so she reads as trusted infrastructure, not only a warm character.

MVP Scope

MoSCoW

Must Have

  • Unified inbox view across channels — single scrollable list, channel badge/icon, read/unread state
  • Maya-drafted replies in conversation view — distinct "drafted" vs. "owner sent" bubbles
  • Owner tone/FAQ setup screen — business info, tone selector, FAQ list
  • Channel connect flow (WhatsApp/IG/FB) — fake "Connect" buttons; Viber/TikTok shown as "Coming soon"

Should Have

  • Urgent-message flagging — red/amber flag on conversations Maya is unsure about or deems urgent
  • Order/booking capture in-chat — a structured "booking card"/"order summary" inline in a conversation

Could Have

  • CRM/lead tracking — basic contact history or tags (e.g. "returning customer," "3 bookings")
  • Multi-agent/team seats — a second "staff" avatar or assignment dropdown, shown but not deeply functional

Won't (Yet)

  • Viber/TikTok integration — coming-soon pills only, no working demo screens
  • Payment processing — no checkout, invoicing, or payment-collection flow
  • Full CRM — no pipeline, deal stages, or reporting/analytics suite

How It's Built

Tech Stack

Frontend

React Native / Expo cross-platform mobile app for iOS and Android — or a simple responsive web app for the MVP demo.

Backend

A managed database + authentication service (e.g. Supabase) for user accounts, channel connections, and API management.

Reply Engine

Conversational engine for drafting replies in the owner's voice, handling FAQs, and flagging urgent/uncertain messages for escalation.

Data

Conversation history and business-context store — FAQs, tone settings, contact history — feeding both Maya's replies and future CRM features.

Design

Figma for interface design, component system, and user-tested prototyping ahead of build.
